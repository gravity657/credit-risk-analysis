# Credit Risk Analysis
Credit risk analysis using Pandas to identify default patterns


## 📌 Objective

This project analyzes a credit risk dataset to identify patterns and insights related to clients' ability to repay credit-based loans. The goal is to understand how different variables influence default risk and to highlight factors that contribute to financial risk for the company.


## 📂 Dataset

-   **Source:** Kaggle
-   **Size:** 1000 rows, 21 columns
-   **Key Features:**
    -   Credit history
    -   Credit amount
    -   Status of savings account/bonds
    -   Status of existing checking account
    -   Default risk


## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

-   Python
-   Pandas
-   Jupyter Notebook
-   Matplotlib+


## 🔍 Project Workflow


### 1. Data Loading and Inspection
Loaded the Credit Risk dataset from a CSV file into a pandas DataFrame and examined its structure to understand the features and overall data layout.

### 2. Data Cleaning
The dataset contained no missing values or major inconsistencies, so no significant cleaning was required. This project focused on understanding the analysis workflow using a clean dataset.

### 3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
Explored variables related to credit risk to identify patterns between high-risk and low-risk clients, particularly in relation to repayment behavior.

### 4. Feature Relationships Analysis
Analyzed relationships between income level, financial strength, employment stability, and default risk to determine which factors most influence a client’s ability to repay loans.

### 5. Key Findings and Interpretation
Lower-income clients showed a higher likelihood of default. While employment stability contributes to repayment ability, external economic factors such as cost of living and inflation also play a role. The analysis suggests the company faces elevated financial risk due to defaults, and risk-based pricing strategies could help improve profitability.



## 📈 Key Insights

- Clients with lower income levels are more likely to default compared to higher-income clients.
- Employment stability influences repayment ability, but it is not the only determining factor.
- External economic conditions (e.g., cost of living, inflation, taxes) significantly impact a client’s financial reliability.
- A high number of defaulters increases the company’s financial risk and reduces profitability.
- Implementing risk-based pricing (higher interest rates for high-risk clients) could help balance potential losses.
